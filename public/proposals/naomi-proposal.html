01 The Facebook group reality

Your 12,000-member Healthy Ageing Project group is your warmest audience, your primary lead funnel, and the thing that takes the most time out of your day. You want an AI agent that can respond to threads as a support persona (not as you), escalate anything sensitive, and post engagement prompts. That is a reasonable ask.

The problem is not the ask. The problem is Meta.

What we found

Meta deprecated the Facebook Groups API in April 2024. That means no third-party tool can officially read posts, write comments, or interact with a Facebook group through any supported channel. Every "Facebook AI agent" on the market today (CommentGuard, Alhena, Tidio, and the rest) operates on Facebook Pages, not Groups. None of them do what you need.

This is not a temporary gap. Meta has been investing heavily in AI for Pages, Messenger, WhatsApp, and Ads, while deliberately restricting developer access to Groups. The trajectory is clear: Groups are being left behind as an automation surface.

Is it technically possible anyway?

Yes, with significant caveats. An AI agent could operate a Facebook group through browser automation: a dedicated computer running a real browser, logged into a dedicated Facebook account that joins the group as a member, reading posts and replying like a human would.

However, we want to be transparent about what that involves:

  • Account warming. A new Facebook account needs 4-6 weeks of gradual activity before it can operate at full capacity without being flagged as spam.
  • Terms of service risk. Automated access to Facebook outside of approved APIs is against Meta's TOS. If Facebook disables the agent's account, it goes offline until a replacement is warmed up.
  • Ongoing maintenance. Facebook's interface changes frequently. When it does, the browser automation breaks and needs manual repair.
  • Hardware requirement. This must run on a physical computer at your location (a Mac mini) on a residential internet connection. Cloud servers are detected and blocked within hours.

Your options

Option 1

Full agentic build

A dedicated Mac mini at your location, running a real browser with its own Facebook account, trained on your cognition, operating as a support persona inside the group 24/7. This is the autonomous solution you described on the call. It is technically possible.

The honest cost: approximately $17,500 AUD including hardware, agent build, account warming, cognition mapping, and ongoing browser maintenance. This reflects the complexity and risk of the build, not a markup.

We are transparent about why we are not recommending this at this stage: the investment is significant, the Facebook TOS risk means the outcome is partially outside our control, and a decaying automation surface is not a foundation we want to build your business on. If this is something you want to pursue in the future, we can revisit it with full knowledge of the trade-offs.

Option 2

Turn on Meta's Group AI

Meta has recently rolled out a native Group AI feature that lets members chat one-on-one with an AI assistant inside the group. The AI answers questions based on the group's historical posts and links back to relevant discussions. It is free, Meta-operated, and carries zero risk to your account.

Check if it is available in your group: go to Group Settings > Admin Tools and look for "Group AI". If it is there, turn it on. If it is not yet available in Australia, it is likely coming soon based on Meta's rollout pattern.

This will not solve everything. It handles FAQ-style questions in a private chat, not thread replies or engagement prompts. But it removes the lowest-value, highest-volume part of your workload at zero cost.

Option 3

Migrate to an email newsletter

This is the medium-term play, and the one we think matters most. Your 12,000 members are your most valuable asset, but they live on a platform that is actively restricting your ability to reach and manage them with AI. Facebook controls the algorithm, the reach, and the rules. An email list is yours.

The play: run a campaign inside the group offering a genuine perk for joining your email list (exclusive content, early cohort access, a free resource). Over 4-8 weeks, migrate the most engaged members onto a newsletter you own. Once they are on your list, your Executive Assistant Agent (the same one we scope in the next section) can help you manage, segment, and respond to that audience with full automation, no platform restrictions, and no risk to your Facebook account.

Email is an open protocol. Unlike Facebook, it is fully agent-compatible. Every reply, every triage decision, every engagement sequence can be handled by an agent that thinks and communicates like you. This is something we could scope alongside the Executive Assistant Agent engagement below, or as a standalone project.
