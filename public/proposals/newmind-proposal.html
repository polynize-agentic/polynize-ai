05 · The transformation

A work unit that compounds.

The point of building a unit (rather than just a tool) is that it gets better with time. The conference is the first decision horizon, but the value continues compounding into Years 2 and 3, as the agents accumulate context, learn what works, and produce outputs you couldn't have produced alone.

Year 1 buys break-even. Year 2 puts the conference into profit. By Year 3 the unit is producing assets, content, and audience growth at a level you couldn't reach with hired hands. The curve doesn't flatten, it accelerates.

The scenario above is illustrative, not promised. Ticket sales depend on programme strength, weather, news cycles, none of which Polynize controls. What we do control is whether the marketing function is actually running in the months leading up to the event, instead of waiting for evenings you don't have. And whether the work done in Year 1 keeps producing in Year 2 and Year 3.
