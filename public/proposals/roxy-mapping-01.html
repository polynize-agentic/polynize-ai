01 · Principles

How we're approaching this.

Three things to know up front so the build runs well.

A
Working out what's automation and what's the agent team
Roxbury's already runs significant automation through Zapier and Zendesk triggers. Part of the work we're doing is figuring out which jobs belong in automation (deterministic, rule-based) and which jobs belong with the agent team (judgement, context, language). That decision shapes the build. We won't replace what's working. We'll add the agent team where automation can't reach.
Principle
B
Roxy gets access like a new hire
She'll have her own Google Workspace identity inside Roxbury's, her own Zendesk agent seat, her own Slack account. Every action she takes is attributed to her. It's cleaner for audit, cleaner for the team's mental model, and makes permission management standard.
Principle
C
Sandbox first, then staged exposure
Before Roxy shows up in the Roxbury's Slack, we'll run her in a private Telegram sandbox. Marrs and Julian test her classifications and tune her. Once she's stable, we'll bring Jaden in to test her, then Scott, against real Roxbury's scenarios. Only then does she deploy to the wider team. By the time the team sees her, she's been through three layers of validation.
Principle
