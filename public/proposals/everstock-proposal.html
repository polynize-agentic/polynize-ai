Full system architecture

The three-team structure

Most businesses deploy agents inside a single function. The EverStock system is designed end-to-end: a Market Validation Team to find and qualify the audience, a Sales Team to convert them, and an Operations Team to deliver and retain them. Each team is a separate build, each builds on the last.

Meet your marketing team

The four agents that make up Phase 1. Scout, Lure, Ember, and Pulse are your Market Validation Team. Sales and Operations are separate builds for later phases, once the market validates. Each agent has a defined role, a domain knowledge base, and a COGNITION.md reasoning protocol installed. Deployed on Polynize Agentic Management infrastructure: AWS Lightsail via OpenClaw, managed through the Agent Console.

AGENT 01
Scout
Content & Social Intelligence
The content arm of the unit. Manages all social channels simultaneously so the human lead never has to. Tests narrative pillars, identifies what is converting, and signals when a pillar is ready to scale. The human decides strategy. Scout executes it continuously.
Content scheduling A/B narrative testing Persona management Engagement tracking
AGENT 02
Lure
Lead Generation & Capture
The lead capture arm of the unit. Runs ManyChat comment-trigger flows, deploys the Household Readiness Quiz, scores and buckets leads by product tier, and routes them to the right funnel. The human lead sees qualified leads. Lure handles every step that gets them there.
ManyChat flows Quiz delivery Lead scoring Landing page routing
AGENT 03
Ember
Website Agent & Inbox
The inbound arm of the unit. Powers live chat on everstock.com.au, trained on EverStock brand voice, product range, and storage network. Pre-qualifies every visitor before the human lead ever sees them. No unqualified inquiry reaches the team. Ember handles it first.
Live chat Brand knowledge base Intent detection Lead handoff
AGENT 04
Pulse
Analytics & Market Signal
The intelligence arm of the unit. Monitors performance across all channels, generates SEO content, and delivers a weekly signal report to the human lead: data, interpretation, and a concrete recommendation. The human lead makes the call. Pulse makes sure it is an informed one.
Cross-channel analytics SEO content Weekly reports Phase 2 recommendation

The cognitive unit: One human lead at Optio directs a layer of agents that handle the high-volume work underneath. Scout, Lure, Ember, and Pulse each have a defined role, a domain knowledge base, and a COGNITION.md reasoning protocol that keeps their output consistent. The human makes the calls. The agents execute, report back, and adapt. That is not four tools running in parallel. It is one coordinated unit with a human at the top.

What the team is worth

What a market validation team really costs, scored across the five dimensions that matter, and three ways to engage.

What the equivalent team costs conventionally

To run the market validation function described in this proposal using a conventional approach, Optio Capital would need to assemble the following team. These are the roles, their realistic fully loaded costs in the Australian market, and what each one still cannot do on its own.

Content & Social Manager
$75–95k / yr
Manages one or two channels. Cannot operate sub-Persona accounts simultaneously. Publishes weekly, not daily. Cannot self-optimise based on signal data.
Growth & Lead Gen Specialist
$70–90k / yr
Builds and monitors funnels. Cannot run ManyChat flows, quiz delivery, and lead scoring simultaneously without dedicated tooling and significant setup time.
Website & Inbound Manager
$60–80k / yr
Handles enquiries during business hours. A 2:30am Brisbane family enquiry goes unanswered until morning. No intent scoring. No automatic lead handoff.
Analytics & Insights Manager
$80–100k / yr
Produces weekly reports but cannot autonomously generate SEO content, cross-reference channel signals, and issue a concrete recommendation in the same workflow.
Platforms, tools & paid media
$3–6k / mo
ManyChat, Meta Ads, analytics stack, email platform, CRM, scheduling tools. Required on top of headcount. No cognitive layer included.
Recruitment & ramp cost
$30–50k one-off
Agency recruitment fees, 3–6 month ramp time per role, onboarding, and knowledge transfer. Paid before a single piece of content is published.

Conventional total: A$315–415k in year one. Four roles fully loaded with super, tools, and recruitment. That figure assumes all four hires perform to brief, ramp without delay, and coordinate effectively across functions from day one. It also assumes the function exists when you need it, not three to six months later.
