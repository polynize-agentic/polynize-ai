The full EverStock agent system spans three teams. Phase 1 builds the Market Validation Team from the ground up, with Sales and Operations scoped for subsequent phases once the market validates.

Full system architecture

The three-team structure

Most businesses deploy agents inside a single function. The EverStock system is designed end-to-end: a Market Validation Team to find and qualify the audience, a Sales Team to convert them, and an Operations Team to deliver and retain them. Each team is a separate build, each builds on the last.

Meet the team

One accountable human lead at Optio. Four agents underneath, handling the volume work. This is the cognitive unit: expert thinking installed into the team, not layered on top. Deployed on Polynize Agentic Management infrastructure: AWS Lightsail via OpenClaw, managed through the Agent Console.

AGENT 01
Scout
Content & Social Intelligence
The content arm of the unit. Manages all social channels simultaneously so the human lead never has to. Tests narrative pillars, identifies what is converting, and signals when a pillar is ready to scale. The human decides strategy. Scout executes it continuously.
Content scheduling A/B narrative testing Persona management Engagement tracking
AGENT 02
Lure
Lead Generation & Capture
The lead capture arm of the unit. Runs ManyChat comment-trigger flows, deploys the Household Readiness Quiz, scores and buckets leads by product tier, and routes them to the right funnel. The human lead sees qualified leads. Lure handles every step that gets them there.
ManyChat flows Quiz delivery Lead scoring Landing page routing
AGENT 03
Ember
Website Agent & Inbox
The inbound arm of the unit. Powers live chat on everstock.com.au, trained on EverStock brand voice, product range, and storage network. Pre-qualifies every visitor before the human lead ever sees them. No unqualified inquiry reaches the team. Ember handles it first.
Live chat Brand knowledge base Intent detection Lead handoff
AGENT 04
Pulse
Analytics & Market Signal
The intelligence arm of the unit. Monitors performance across all channels, generates SEO content, and delivers a weekly signal report to the human lead: data, interpretation, and a concrete recommendation. The human lead makes the call. Pulse makes sure it is an informed one.
Cross-channel analytics SEO content Weekly reports Phase 2 recommendation

The cognitive unit: One human lead at Optio directs a layer of agents that handle the high-volume work underneath. Scout, Lure, Ember, and Pulse each have a defined role, a domain knowledge base, and a COGNITION.md reasoning protocol that keeps their output consistent. The human makes the calls. The agents execute, report back, and adapt. That is not four tools running in parallel. It is one coordinated unit with a human at the top.

A day with the team

The EverStock Market Validation Agent Team in action. Here is what a typical week looks like once the build is live.

Three scenarios from a real week in the field. Four agents, one shared mission: find out whether Australia is ready to buy preparedness. Calmly, practically, at scale.

Monday 7:00am · Week launch SCOUT + PULSE
Scout → The Optio team
"Morning. Week two is live. I've published 36 pieces across Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, and YouTube, split across the main EverStock account and your four Persona sub-accounts. Pulse flagged the 'family insurance' angle as the strongest under-tested frame from last week: 'Most families insure their home, but not their essentials.' I've queued that as the lead hook on Facebook this week, and set up ManyChat triggers on the TikTok 'are you prepared?' post. Anyone who comments gets routed to the Household Readiness Quiz automatically."
Scout manages all channels simultaneously, running the EverStock brand account alongside sub-Persona accounts targeting family preparedness, urban convenience, bushfire-country households, and high-conviction planners. Pulse feeds Scout the winning angles from engagement data. No manual rebriefing required.
Wednesday 2:30pm · Inbound enquiry EMBER + LURE
Visitor → Ember (via everstock.com.au)
"Hi, we're a family of four in Brisbane. We've been thinking about this for a while. What's the storage situation? We don't have room at home for a year's worth of supplies."
Ember → Visitor
"That's one of the main reasons EverStock exists. For families thinking long-term, we store your supplies for you: climate-controlled, secure, and accessible near Brisbane when you need them. You don't keep anything at home. We recommend the Home System for a family of four: three months of supply, stored locally, with the option to extend. Want me to send through the details and add you to the early-access waitlist? I can have something in your inbox in two minutes."
Ember handled this entire query. It captured intent (family of 4, Brisbane, storage-sensitive, mid-tier product fit), qualified the lead, and handed structured data to Lure, who tagged them as a priority Home System prospect and routed them to the Brisbane landing page funnel. No human touch required until outreach.
Friday 5:00pm · Week wrap PULSE
Pulse → The Optio team
"Week 2 summary. Reached: 1,840 people across channels. Quiz completions: 103. Of those, 72 qualified into your ICP. Waitlist additions: 41. Strongest-performing angle: 'Preparedness without storing anything at home' on Facebook, family audience, 4.1x higher click-through than the supply-chain angle. Weakest: TikTok doom framing, low completion rate. Recommendation: cut doom-angle, double Facebook family and storage convenience content in Week 3. The signal is real and pointing at the Home System as the primary conversion offer. Suggest we discuss paid budget reallocation before Monday."
Pulse delivers this every Friday. Data, interpretation, recommendation. Not just a dashboard to stare at. By week two you already know which Persona is winning, which angle is converting, and where to put next week's spend.
