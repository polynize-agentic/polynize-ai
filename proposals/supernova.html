01 Purpose

This pre-proposal outlines a focused first implementation for Supernova: a Due Diligence Agent Team designed to support first-pass investment review on promising companies entering the funnel.

The aim is to start with a high-value, self-contained part of the workflow that can deliver immediate leverage and benefit for the investment lead, while creating a strong foundation for future expansion if useful.

02 The Opportunity

Supernova operates across founder support, advisor matching, and selective capital deployment. That makes the full operating environment powerful, but also complex.

A strong first implementation is to build a dedicated Due Diligence Agent Team around first-pass review.

Current due diligence tooling is often too generic, too rigid, or too expensive. It tends to automate steps rather than support dynamic judgment. For Supernova, the requirement is not a fixed checklist. It is a system that can adapt its review based on the company being evaluated.

"There must be intelligence to make decisions and customise the criteria in real time so that you can properly evaluate a company. It cannot just be step one, step two, step three."

Lorenzo de Leo, Supernova

03 The Agent Team

The proposed Due Diligence Agent Team is designed to take:

  • pitch deck
  • financial summary or model
  • core data room materials
  • optional meeting notes

and produce:

  • a tailored diligence framework
  • structured findings by stream
  • open issues / missing information
  • an editable memo draft for the investment lead

This is not workflow automation. It is a coordinated agent team supporting dynamic, stage-aware, vertical-aware investment review. The architecture follows the logic already discussed: an orchestrator, specialist diligence streams, and a QA / synthesis layer.

Team Design

Role Core Job Main Output
Investment Lead / DD Lead Owns framing, judgment, red-flag interpretation, and next-step decision. Final review and decision.
Orchestrator Determines what matters for this company based on stage and vertical; briefs the specialist agents. Tailored diligence framework and stream briefs.
Market Agent Reviews market size, competition, comparables, positioning, and trends. Market findings, risks, open questions.
Financial Agent Reviews business model, burn, runway, pricing logic, forecast sanity, and financial health. Financial findings, inconsistencies, risks.
Team Agent Reviews founder and team quality, execution credibility, role coverage, and operating maturity. Team findings, strengths, gaps, key risks.
Legal / Compliance Agent Surfaces structure, cap table basics, regulatory, legal, and compliance flags. Legal / compliance flags and counsel review items.
QA / Synthesis Agent Reconciles outputs, detects contradictions, identifies weak evidence, and compiles the final draft. Integrated memo draft, gap list, follow-up questions.

04 Operating Flow

The flow from deal intake to decision support, end to end.

Step What Happens Output
01 / Deal Intake Investment lead uploads deck and core materials. Deal workspace created.
02 / Dynamic Framing Orchestrator determines what matters by stage and vertical. Tailored diligence framework.
03 / Parallel Specialist Review Market, Financial, Team, and Legal / Compliance agents review in parallel. Findings by stream.
04 / Reconciliation QA / Synthesis agent checks consistency, gaps, and evidence quality. Integrated memo draft and open issues.
05 / Human Review Investment lead reviews and decides next step. Pass, pursue, or request more info.

05 Indicative Leverage

A Due Diligence Agent Team like this creates value by compressing the path from company materials to structured decision support. For a lean investment team, the value is not only time saved. It is increased deal-handling capacity, better use of senior judgment, and a shorter path from intake to structured output.

Dimension With Due Diligence Agent Team
First-pass diligence cycle time Structured parallel review and synthesis rather than fragmented manual assembly.
Use of senior investment time More time concentrated on judgment, exceptions, and next-step decisions.
Coverage across diligence streams Market, financial, team, and legal / compliance review in parallel.
Effective review capacity per lead Indicatively 3 to 5x greater first-pass diligence leverage once calibrated to the team's real process.

A reasonable working assumption is that a system like this should deliver materially faster first-pass diligence, better use of senior investment attention, more consistent output across opportunities, and 3 to 5x effective leverage in first-pass diligence capacity for the same core human bandwidth.

07 Scope Approach

The cleanest path is a staged approach. Stage 1 locks the diligence logic with the Supernova team. Stage 2 deploys the agent team that runs on top of it.

Stage 01

Framework Discovery

Working sessions to lock in Supernova's diligence logic, how criteria vary by stage and vertical, how signal is weighed, and what a strong memo should look like. Output: a documented framework that becomes the spec for the build.

1 week
Stage 02

Agent Team Deployment

Build and configure the Due Diligence Agent Team, including the orchestrator, specialist agents, QA / synthesis layer, a lightweight deal workspace, and the memo generation flow. This keeps the implementation practical while grounding the system in how Supernova actually thinks.

3 to 5 weeks
